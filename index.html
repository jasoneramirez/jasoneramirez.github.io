I am a postdoctoral researcher with a background in mathematics, currently based in Montréal. I hold undergraduate degrees in Mathematics and Physics, as well as an MSc in Mathematics. I completed my PhD in Mathematics at the University of Seville, under the co-supervision of Emilio Carrizosa and Dolores Romero Morales.

My research initially focused on counterfactual explanations, where I worked on extending individual-level explanations to a group-level perspective, motivated by a stakeholder’s view. This global perspective enables a more insightful understanding of how models behave, including the detection of biases and patterns, and led me to explore interpretability analysis approaches beyond standard predictive models, such as data envelopment analysis (DEA).

More recently, my focus has shifted toward explaining decisions arising from data-driven and stochastic optimization models. Rather than explaining only predictions or classification outcomes, I am interested in understanding how optimization-based decisions are made under uncertainty, including both endogenous and exogenous sources of uncertainty.

Outside academia, I keep my artistic side alive through music. I hold a Professional Title in Piano and have completed three years of advanced studies. I also am an avid reader and lover cinema, recommendations and thoughtful discussions are always welcome!

Selected Research

Mathematical Optimization Modelling for Group Counterfactual Explanations

European Journal of Operational Research, 2024

This paper introduces a mathematical optimization framework for generating counterfactual explanations at the group level. Moving beyond individual explanations, we adopt a stakeholder perspective in which counterfactuals must be produced jointly under structural and feasibility constraints. The resulting models reveal close connections between group counterfactual explanations and classical facility location problems.

Counterfactual Analysis and Target Setting in Benchmarking

European Journal of Operational Research, 2024

We extend counterfactual reasoning to Data Envelopment Analysis (DEA), introducing an optimization-based framework for interpretable target setting in efficiency analysis. Counterfactual targets are computed via mixed-integer convex optimization, providing transparent and actionable explanations for inefficient units in benchmarking applications.

Generating Collective Counterfactual Explanations in Score-Based Classification via Mathematical Optimization

Expert Systems with Applications, 2024

This work studies collective counterfactual explanations, where explanations are generated for an entire group of individuals at the same time. We formulate the problem as a mixed-integer optimization model that minimizes total perturbation cost while guaranteeing a favorable classification outcome, allowing shared influential features and outliers to be identified simultaneously.

Teaching

Linear Models and Design of Experiments

BSc in Mathematics & Mathematics–Statistics (3rd–4th year), University of Seville

I taught practical sessions focused on linear regression models and experimental design, with an emphasis on implementation and data analysis in R. My teaching approach centered on helping students translate theoretical concepts into working statistical models, write reproducible code, and critically interpret outputs. Particular attention was given to model diagnostics, experimental structure, and the connection between assumptions and results.

Statistics

BSc in Health Engineering (1st year), University of Seville

I taught both theory and practice in an introductory statistics course for engineering students. Alongside foundational topics in descriptive statistics, probability, and inference, I introduced students to statistical computing in R, focusing on hands-on data analysis and interpretation. The goal was to equip students with practical tools to analyze real data and to understand statistical reasoning in applied health and engineering contexts.
