About

I am a postdoctoral researcher with a background in mathematics, currently based in Montréal. I hold undergraduate degrees in Mathematics and Physics, as well as an MSc in Mathematics. I completed my PhD in Mathematics at the University of Seville, under the co-supervision of Emilio Carrizosa and Dolores Romero Morales. During my doctoral training, I also carried out research visits at Copenhagen Business School and Cornell Tech.

My research began with an interest in counterfactual explanations, where I worked on extending individual-level explanations to a group-level perspective motivated by a stakeholders' view. Adopting a global viewpoint allowed for a more informative analysis of model behavior, as well as the identification of biases and recurring patterns. This line of work naturally led me to explore explanatory approaches beyond standard predictive models, including applications to data envelopment analysis (DEA).

More recently, my focus has shifted toward explaining decisions arising from data-driven and stochastic optimization models. Rather than limiting explanations to predictions or classification outcomes, I am interested in understanding how optimization-based decisions are made under uncertainty, including both endogenous and exogenous sources of uncertainty.

Alongside my research, I value contributing to the academic community and promoting open scientific practices. I am a strong advocate of open science, including free and open-source code, open access publications, and transparent research workflows.

Beyond academia, I have always tried to keep space for interests outside mathematics. Alongside my scientific training, I pursued formal studies in music and hold a Professional Title in Piano, completing three years of advanced studies. I am also an enthusiastic reader and cinema lover, and I enjoy conversations that challenge ideas and perspectives. Recommendations and thoughtful discussions are always welcome!
